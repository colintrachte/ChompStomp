# Chomp Stomp

## Why This Exists

This game is being built for two specific kids, not for a market. This document is for humans. 

Every major design decision comes from what Chloe and Ryan actually enjoy, not from genre conventions, monetization, retention metrics, or what would appeal to the average player.

If a feature makes the game better for random players but worse for Chloe and Ryan, the feature loses.

---

# The Two Kids

## Chloe

* Builder.
* Precision player.
* Likes creating things.
* Wants to customize everything.
* Better at games than Ryan.
* Hates losing to Ryan.
* Bored of Slither.io because it lacks progression and goals.

Most important request:

> "Can you make it so that we can customize the worm however we like?"

The Worm Builder is not a side feature.

It is one of the pillars of the entire game.

---

## Ryan

* Likes Minecraft.
* Likes Sonic.
* Loves Powder Toy.
* Likes funny outcomes.
* Likes discovery.

Ryan does not want explosions handed to him.

He wants ingredients that can be combined to create surprising outcomes.

The game should reward experimentation.

---

# Core Truth

The original assumption was wrong.

This is not a versus-first game.

It is a solo-first game.

The daily loop is:

* play alone
* eat
* grow
* fight enemies
* beat bosses
* unlock things
* customize the worm

Versus is a special event.

Co-op bosses are a special event.

Solo play must be fun enough that Chloe would choose it instead of Slither.io.

---

# The Fairness Rule

This is the most important rule in the project.

## Grind Buys Breadth, Never Height

Solo progression may unlock:

* new parts
* new faces
* new abilities
* new creature forms
* new blueprints
* new companions

Solo progression may not unlock:

* more damage
* more health
* more speed
* larger starting size
* better stats

Grinding should make a worm more personal.

Grinding should never make a worm objectively stronger against a sibling.

---

# The Worm Belongs To The Kid

Every major system should increase the feeling that:

> This is MY worm.

Examples:

* colors
* shapes
* faces
* companions
* unlocks
* blueprints
* creature evolution
* cosmetic transformations

The game succeeds when a kid instantly recognizes their worm from across the room.

---

# The Body Is The Scoreboard

No score.

No XP bar.

No crown.

No floating numbers.

A kid should know who is winning by looking at the worm.

Growth and evolution are visible.

The worm itself communicates success.

---

# Losing Must Be Funny

Losing is allowed.

Losing should happen.

But losing should be funny.

Examples:

* blasted off screen
* turned into a balloon
* popped out the other end
* scattered into beads
* flattened
* deflated

The goal is not to prevent frustration.

The goal is to make defeat entertaining.

---

# Ryan's Discovery Rule

The game should feel like:

> I combined things and something happened.

Not:

> I pressed the explosion button.

A small set of hidden properties combines to create emergent effects.

The player thinks in items.

The engine thinks in tags.

---

# The Secret Layer

Creature care.

Gardens.

Companions.

Cooperation.

These systems exist.

But they are not the main game.

The main game is eating, growing, fighting, and winning.

The secret systems quietly make the world richer.

---

# Karma

Care and cooperation should reliably create delightful outcomes.

The game should never announce this.

The world simply behaves differently.

Players should gradually notice the pattern.

Karma creates delight.

Never power.

---

# What Done Looks Like

A single worm.

Solo play.

Eat food.

Grow.

Fight enemies.

Beat a boss.

Unlock a new part.

Customize the worm.

If Chloe would replay that instead of Slither.io, the game exists.

Everything else comes afterward.

## 2. Cleaned-Up & Spell-Checked Version

### **Game Title: The Worms Eat**

#### **Core Mechanics & Customization**
*   "Can you make it look like this?"
*   "And can you make it so that we can customize/make the worm however we like it?"
*   "Can you make it look like this? This goes too!"

#### **Face Abilities & Food Transformations**
*   **The Face with the Tongue Sticking Out:** Transforms the food into lollipops, then goes back to normal.
*   **The Orange Face:** Transforms the food into oranges, then goes back to normal.
*   **The Cat Face:** Transforms the food into paws, then goes back to normal.
*   **The Bunny Face:** Does the exact same thing as the cat (transforms food into bunny paws/treats, then goes back to normal).
*   **The Fish Tank / Fish Face:** Transforms the food into goldfish, and it stays that way permanently.
*   **The Winking Face:** Does nothing.
*   **The Egg Face:** Transforms the food into bacon, then goes back to normal.
*   **The Butterfly Face:** Does nothing.
*   **The Astronaut Face:** Transforms the food into moons, then goes back to normal.
